## 1. Technical SEO — what's broken

### Critical (fix this week)

1. **No `sitemap.xml`** — Google can crawl the site without it, but indexing is faster and more reliable with one. *Effort: 5 min.*
2. **No `robots.txt`** — Without this, search engines have no explicit crawl directives. Even a permissive 3-liner helps. *Effort: 2 min.*
3. **No structured data (JSON-LD)** — Missing `LocalBusiness`, `Service`, and `FAQPage` schema. This is the single biggest GEO/AI-engine signal you're missing — AI engines like Perplexity, ChatGPT browse, and Google's AI Overviews heavily prefer pages with schema. *Effort: 30 min.*
4. **No `<link rel="canonical">` tags** — Risk of duplicate-content dilution between `/` and `/index.html`. *Effort: 5 min.*
5. **No Open Graph / Twitter Card tags** — Site previews on LinkedIn / X / Facebook show as bare URLs. *Effort: 10 min.*
6. **Six of seven pages have no `meta description`** — only `index.html` has one. Subpages will get auto-generated snippets which are usually worse. *Effort: 15 min.*

### Important (fix this month)

7. Lucide icons loaded from `unpkg.com/lucide@latest` — unpinned version is both a security risk (supply-chain) and a perf hit (no long-term cache). Pin to a specific version and self-host or use a versioned CDN URL.
8. Google Fonts loaded render-blocking — add `display=swap` is present, but consider `font-display: optional` for hero or self-host Inter.
9. No `<link rel="preload">` for the hero image (`images/hero.png`) — LCP candidate.
10. No favicon / apple-touch-icon declared.
11. Header has duplicate nav block for mobile menu — accessibility cleanup.
12. No `lang` attribute issues found (good — `lang="en"` is set).

## 2. On-page — content audit

### Strengths
- Title tags are descriptive and include "LA" / Los Angeles.
- H1s are unique per page.
- Hero copy mentions NFPA 72, NICET, California fire code — strong topical authority signals.
- Phone number visible in header (good for mobile conversions).

### Gaps
- **Single areas page** for four counties. Competitors like FireServicePro have dedicated city pages (`/Santa-Monica/`, `/Torrance/`, etc.) — these rank for "fire alarm inspection [city]" long-tail queries.
- **Compliance FAQ exists** but isn't marked up with `FAQPage` schema, so it can't earn rich results.
- **No blog or resources** section. Competitors (uptocode.build, kimblefire.com) win AI-engine citations through deep guide content (NFPA 72 inspection requirements, testing frequencies, cost guides).
- **Internal linking is shallow** — services.html doesn't link to each individual service page (annual-testing, maintenance, lighting). Crawlers and users both lose.
- **Testimonials are generic** ("Sarah J., Mark T.") — Google's E-E-A-T guidelines prefer full names + company / verifiable sources.

## 3. GEO / AI engine optimization

**Good news first:** A web search for "best fire alarm testing company Los Angeles 2026 reviews" returned FireAlarmTestingPro in the AI-generated summary alongside American Fire and Spectrum Fire Protection. The site is already on AI engines' radar.

**Why you're getting picked up:** Clean copy, explicit NFPA 72 / NICET / California fire code mentions, and a clear LA service-area declaration.

**What's holding you back from being cited more:**
1. No `LocalBusiness` or `Service` JSON-LD — AI engines can't extract structured facts (hours, service area polygon, services, ratings) cleanly.
2. No FAQ schema on compliance.html — AI engines love FAQ schema for direct Q&A pulls.
3. No author bio / about / certifications page — E-E-A-T weakness for ChatGPT / Claude / Perplexity which prefer answers from clearly authoritative sources.
4. Thin content footprint — competitors have 50-100+ indexed pages; you have 7.
